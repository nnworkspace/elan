# Stakeholders in the Digital Euro Project

This list identifies the major categories of stakeholders involved in the Digital Euro project and explains their roles. While the precise composition of stakeholder groups and working bodies may evolve, the categories below reflect current public engagement and institutional design as communicated by the European Central Bank (ECB) and related bodies.

| Stakeholder Group                                                                 | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **European Central Bank (ECB)**                                                   | The central authority driving the Digital Euro project, responsible for design choices, rulebook preparation, coordination with EU institutions, and ensuring alignment with monetary policy objectives and financial stability. ([European Central Bank][1])             |
| **Eurosystem National Central Banks (NCBs)**                                      | The central banks of euro area countries that participate in the project, contribute to the rulebook development, experimentation, and technical analyses, and support execution within their jurisdictions. ([European Central Bank][1])                                 |
| **Payment Service Providers (PSPs)**                                              | Banks and licensed payment institutions that will distribute digital euro services to end users (individuals and merchants) and integrate scheme interfaces into their platforms. ([European Central Bank][2])                                                            |
| **Merchants and Merchant Organisations**                                          | Commercial stakeholders whose acceptance infrastructure and user experience considerations are essential for day-to-day digital euro payments; engaged through user research and rulebook feedback. ([European Central Bank][1])                                          |
| **Consumers / Citizens**                                                          | End users of the Digital Euro whose needs, preferences (e.g., privacy, usability), and accessibility requirements are considered in design and pilots; included via research and focus groups. ([European Central Bank][1])                                               |
| **Digital Euro Scheme Rulebook Development Group (RDG)**                          | A multi-stakeholder group convened to gather market input and industry perspectives on the draft rulebook’s functional and technical requirements, including representatives from merchants, PSPs, banks, and third-party service providers. ([European Central Bank][3]) |
| **Innovation Platform Participants**                                              | A broader set of market participants — including fintechs, start-ups, academia, and technology providers — that collaborate on use cases, experimentation, and piloting activities. ([European Central Bank][4])                                                          |
| **EU Institutions (European Commission, European Parliament, Council of the EU)** | The co-legislators responsible for passing the legal framework (Regulation) that will establish the Digital Euro. They engage with the ECB through technical input and workshops. ([European Central Bank][1])                                                            |
| **Market Advisory and Expert Groups**                                             | Advisory bodies (such as earlier Market Advisory Groups) that provide strategic, industry, or technical advice to the ECB on distribution models, scheme design, and market conditions. ([European Central Bank][5])                                                      |
| **Vendors and Technical Providers**                                               | Third-party service providers contracted (sometimes under NDA) to build specific components of the Digital Euro infrastructure (e.g., fraud detection, platform modules) as guided by RFPs and procurement frameworks. ([European Central Bank][1])                       |
